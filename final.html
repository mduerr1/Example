Top Ten NHL Goalies

2017-2018 season

A ranking of the ten best goalies from the 2017-2018 season. Based on statistics from NHL.com

Each goalie has a link to their statistics and team website.

Number 10: Antti Raanta (Arizona)

Although Antti Raanta is a young and somewhat inexperienced goalie, he had an excelent season. Especially considering the fact that he played for one of the worst teams in the league. In addition to making some spectacular saves, he also managed to steal 3 shutouts and boasts a 2.24 GAA, tied for second best in the league.

Number 9: Jonathan Quick (Los Angeles)

Consistantly the most flexible goaltender in the NHL, Quick had yet another impressive season with a team that did not have the stongest of offenses. The Kings led the league in lowest goals against, thanks in large part to their William M. Jennings award winning goaltender.

Number 8: Sergei Bobrovsky (Columbus)

Currently the only two-time Vezina award winner active in the NHL, Sergei Bobrovsky continues to accumulate wins in the toughest division in hockey. Starting in a total of 65 games this season, he was able to handle one of the heavier workloads amongst all goalies.

Number 7: Frederik Andersen (Toronto)

This year Andersen and the Maple Leafs broke and set new franchise records for most wins and points in a season. Although sometimes inconsistent, when he is on his game, Andersen is nearly imposible to beat.

Number 6: Braden Holtby (Washington)

Although he had a bit of an off year, Braden Holtby has had the best postseason of his career. He led his team to the Stanley Cup Final for the first time since 1999. His ability to move laterally is among his best attributes. Holtby showcased his talents in the Eastern Conference Final where he shutout the Lightning for the last two and a half games.

Number 5: Tuukka Rask (Boston)

Number 4: Connor Hellebuyck (Winnipeg)

One of the largest goalies in the league at 6'4", Hellebuyck emerged this year as a top five net-minder. He led his team to their first playoff series win and to the Western Conference final. He also set franchise records in wins and save percentage. Additionally, he is a finalist for the Vezina trophy, the award for the best goaltender throughout the regular season.

Number 3: Andrei Vasilevskiy (Tampa Bay)

Finally earning the role of starter, the 23-year-old uses his size and flexibility to make sound, but uniquely creative saves. Vasilevskiy set franchise records for wins and shutouts while he led the lightning to the Eastern Conference Final and stood on his head to keep his team in the series. Deservedly so, he has also been nominateded for the Vezina trophy.

Number 2: Marc-Andre Fluery (Las Vegas)

After being taken by Vegas in the expansion draft Fluery has had yet another career year. Although he suffered from injury early in the season he was able to return and lead the Knights to first place in the Pacific Division. Additionally, he has transended to a new level of goaltending throughout these playoffs. Currently with an unheard of GAA and save percentage, Fluery is on track to win his third consecutive Stanley Cup. If he does so, he would most certainly win the Conn Smythe Trophy as the playoff MVP.

Number 1: Pekka Rinne (Nashville)

With his ability to cover the lower half of the net as well as his strong skating, Pekka Rinne was simply sensational for much of the past season. In a 23 game span, the Finn went 21-1-1, ultimately leading to career highs in wins and shutouts. He also finished the season as a Vezina finalist and President's trophy winner.
